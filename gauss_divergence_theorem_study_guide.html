1 The Big Idea ~5 min

As it actually is (exam version)

Let S be a closed surface enclosing a volume V. If FŐĄ is a continuously differentiable vector point function, then:

The total flux of FŐĄ leaving through the closed surface S equals the total divergence (outflow-per-point) added up over the entire volume V inside.

In short: what leaks out through the skin = what's being generated inside.

Like you're 5

Imagine a closed room full of kids blowing up balloons and letting them go ‚ÄĒ puff! ‚ÄĒ into the air.

You could chase down every single puff of air inside the room and add them up. Or, you could just stand at the door and windows and count all the air breezing past you on its way out.

Both counts give the exact same number. Puffs made inside = air that escapes outside.

3 Why It's True ‚ÄĒ Picture It ~5 min

The trick behind the proof

Chop volume V into tiny cubes. Each tiny cube leaks a bit of flux out of its 6 faces.

When two tiny cubes sit side by side, the flux leaving one face exactly cancels the flux entering its neighbor's matching face (equal and opposite) ‚ÄĒ so all the internal faces cancel out.

Only the outer faces ‚ÄĒ the ones on the boundary S ‚ÄĒ survive. So: sum of tiny outflows (divergence, everywhere inside) = flux through only the outside skin.
